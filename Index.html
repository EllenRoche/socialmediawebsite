Website Porpose

This website is to showcase how easy it is for you, a student or teacher to incorporate and use social media tools for both learning and teaching in third level education. This is for third level students of any age or any ability level. You can us this site to learn about how each individual social media tool I chose can help you in different aspects of learning.  It will also show how education is evolving and how learning can be done online and in unique ways. It will also show how students and teachers can stay connected when their education is interrupted, like we are currently experiencing ourselves.  I will also show you how quick and easy these social media tools are to install, register and use. The five tools you will read about are Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, Slack and WhatsApp. 

Five Tools

Facebook

Facebook is an American social media service created by Mark Zuckerberg and his roommates, I'm sure you've all seen the social networking, if not it’s a good watch. It was launched the 4th of February 2004, 16 years ago.  It now has over 2.50 billion monthly active users. It is a great way to stay in touch with friends and family.  

Twitter

Twitter is an American social media service but for microblogging. Users can like, post and retweet posts. It was founded on the 21st of March 2006 by Jack Dorsey. It has 321 million active users. It is a popular platform for celebrities and politicians. Even Donal Trump is on twitter.  

YouTube

YouTube is an American online video-sharing platform. It was created by three PayPal employees in 2005, it is now owned by Google. You can find a video on just about everything on this site. More than one billion hours of YouTube content is watched daily. You can rent movies and listen to Music. Anyone can upload to YouTube, many people have made a career with this, doing adverts and working with brands on their videos. 

Slack

Slack launched in August of 20015 slack is the newest social media tool we will cover. It is used for instant messaging on public and private channels. Slack has partnered with financial and human capital management firm Workday. It has over 12 million active users in over 150 countries.  

WhatsApp

WhatsApp is not a social website; it is just a social media app for an android or iOS phone. More that 2 billion people in over 180 countries use WhatsApp. It was founded by Jan Koum and Brian Acton who worked for Yahoo. It also joined Facebook in 2014. WhatsApp is a pun for What's Up.
