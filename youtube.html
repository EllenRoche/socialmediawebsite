About

  • YouTube is an American online video sharing platform that aloows users to upload, view, rate, share, add to playlists, report, comment on videos, and subscribe to other users.
  • The majority of videos are free to view, but there are exceptions, like subscription-based channels, film rentals, as well as YouTube Music and YouTube Premium, subscription services offering premium and ad-free music streaming, and ad-free access to all content.
  • YouTube, LLC is a billion dollar company and selected creators earn a lot of money in advertising revenue from Google AdSense.

Educational Use -Students/ Teachers

Educational use of YouTube for both students and teachers

  • YouTube can be a very useful social media tool for both third level students and educators, especially during times like this when students and educators can’t attend their regular weekly lectures. Lecturers can record lectures at home and upload them to YouTube. Students can then watch the lecture as many times and whenever they want. They can also reference and refer to lectures posted when doing any assigned work. Students can ask questions and talk in the comments. Lecturers can read and reply to comments. Students can record presentations at home to upload for the lecturer to see. They can also upload and video related projects. Students can also use YouTube for research purposes for any subject.
